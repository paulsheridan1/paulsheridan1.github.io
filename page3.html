What Is A Help Line?

. . .

A help line is a service which provides listening and emotional support to anyone in distress, in an individual, family or psycho-social crisis, who is asking for support, and could be feeling lonely, isolated, unhappy, frightened, worried, in shock or suicidal. Services should be urgent, non-judgmental, empathetic, respectful, caring and provided by trained volunteers or staff. They can offer access to emotional support in a number of ways Telephone Email Chat Drop-in centres (face-to-face) Outreach programmes in the community Websites for people to access self-help and other information in local languages Self-help and other information in different formats, such as leaflets, videos, CD Roms and DVDs

Why Should You Utilise This?

. . .

We suggest that you call the youth helpline which is a highly confidential company which will provide you with free professional advice and help which will guide you on the right track to avoid futher problems along the road.
